# NOOP v8.5.1

A tuning-and-clarity release: HRV you can compare to WHOOP, workout auto-detect that keeps the warm-up, a Fitness Age card that scores instead of stalling, optional bar charts in Trends, and clearer Home icons. Update from the Releases page, AltStore, or `brew upgrade`.

## Added

- **HRV deep-sleep window (#141).** NOOP computed nightly HRV as the whole-night RMSSD mean across all sleep stages, while WHOOP reports it from the last slow-wave (deep) sleep window — so NOOP read ~1.5–2× higher. A new **Settings → HRV window → Deep sleep** option scores HRV over the deep-sleep window to match WHOOP's methodology. Whole-night stays the default; switching re-anchors the HRV baseline so Charge compares like-for-like (a few nights to settle). A Test Centre HRV trace surfaces the whole-night vs deep-only figures so you can confirm the fit against your own data. Both platforms.
- **Trends line/bar chart toggle (#134).** A Settings option renders the Trends graphs as bar charts (anchored to zero) rather than lines. Both platforms.
- **Fitness Age refresh button (#142).** Force an immediate recompute instead of waiting for the next scheduled pass.

## Fixed

- **Workout auto-detect keeps the warm-up (#148).** A workout needs both elevated heart rate *and* motion, but heart rate lags motion at the onset of an effort — so the first 10–15 minutes of a walk or ride (moving, HR still climbing) were clipped, while the way back tracked from the first step because HR was already up. A confirmed workout's start now back-dates over the contiguous warm-up motion that preceded it, without crossing a genuine rest. Both platforms.
- **Fitness Age no longer shows "No Data" with all inputs present (#140).** The card gated on this-pass computed nights and starved when your resting-HR came from imported daily aggregates; it now reads the persisted history the card actually shows. Both platforms.
- **Fitness Age card shows the "N more nights" countdown (#139).** Instead of a dead-end empty gauge, the card now tells you how many more nights of wear it needs.
- **Distinct Hydration icon (#150).** Hydration and Blood Oxygen rendered the identical Home/Trends tile — same glyph and same tint. Hydration now has its own drink-vessel icon. Both platforms.

## Notes

- The Deep-sleep HRV window is opt-in and non-destructive: your history is kept, and toggling it simply re-scores recent nights and re-learns your Charge baseline over the following few nights. Because Body Age scores HRV against a deep-sleep-based age norm, switching to the deep-sleep window also makes Body Age slightly more accurate.
